A/B/C-test

A/B testing is a term for a randomized experiment with two variants, A and B, and in this case also a C version which are the control and variation in the controlled experiment. A/B/C-testing is when 33% of the users get devided in to one of the versions. When the test has been live for a longer period of time you have a result about which one has been the more succesfull version according to your goals.

After we gathered information from the user test and the web guidelines , I focused on identifying design concepts, design examples and the language used on their webpage that was all grounded in the user analysis. This was presented as a workshow where the cutomers and design group gathred to go though the design examples for the A/B/C-test to make sure the customers felt like the design was applicable and according to the user needs. 


An agenda could look like this:

We describe what we found and what we want to highlight as important things to work on.

The customer gives his idea of ​​what they identified as important and as they are working on trying to solve.

We discuss and prioritize and try to differentiate between what we agree and where solutions can be implemented in a new design, such as the need for a job base and something that may be important but that we set aside for this time.

The goal of the process was to identify what leads to:

Increased sales

Fewer errors (but not necessarily more sales)

Reduced support needs
