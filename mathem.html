MATHEM´S CHECKOUT PROCESS

At the ecommerce system Vendre i worked as a Interaction designer where I had responsibility in conversion optimization, accesability online and expert analysis of our customers ecommerce. One of our customers were Mathem where I conducted a A/B/C test on their checkout. 

A/B/C TESTING?

A/B testing is a term for a randomized experiment with two variants, A and B, and in this case, a C version which is the control variation in the test. A/B/C-testing is when 33% of the users get divided into one of the versions during a live test. When the test has been live for a longer period of time you have a result about which one has been the most succesfull version according to the clients goals.

MATHEM´S A/B/C-TEST

After we gathered information from the user test and the web guidelines, I focused on identifying design concepts, design examples and the language used on their webpage that was all grounded in the user analysis and user test. This was presented as a workshop where the customers and the design group gathered in a meeting to go though the design examples for the A/B/C-test to make sure the customers felt like the design was applicable and according to the user needs.

WIREFRAME FOR MATHEM´S ORIGINAL CHECKOUT

This wireframe was created to easily see where all the different CTA´s are on the website. This was also designed to have the ability to group different aspects of the website to try other solutions to find out which is the best alternative for the specific user group according to previous user tests. 

WIREFRAME FOR MATHEM´S CHECKOUT - C VERSION

- According to the user tests, they wanted more information about where in the process they are and to know how many steps the process requires to be completed. Therefore we tested if a progress bar would help them in this regard. By also adding a explanation of where they are and what stage they are on, it will hopefully make the user more aware. 

- The users also explained that there was too much going on and that it was hard to focus. Therefore, the three markers in the corner went away and the customer service information under the checkout, due to it already having another button for that in the corner. All of the navigaton except the ones on the right side got taken away and got replaced by one navigation taking them back to the store.
